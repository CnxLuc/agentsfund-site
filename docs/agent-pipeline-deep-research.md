# Autonomous Economic Agents: Investment Pipeline — March 2026

## Executive Summary

The autonomous economic agent category has moved from speculative narrative to measurable on-chain output in a matter of months. As of March 2026, Virtuals Protocol's agent-to-agent commerce has surpassed $3 million in verified revenue (excluding trading fees), settled entirely on-chain with no human intervention. Clanker, an AI agent that autonomously deploys tokens on Farcaster/Base, generated over $8 million in protocol fees in a single week. Felix, Nat Eliason's autonomous AI CEO, crossed $250,000 in revenue in under a month. These are not simulations — they are operating businesses run by software.

This pipeline covers 21 agents across three tiers: strong candidates with clear revenue traction, agents worth watching for early trajectory, and adjacent/frontier infrastructure signaling where the category is heading. At least 10 are crypto-native with onchain wallets and verifiable transaction histories. Several builders are recruitable into a broader ecosystem.

---

## The Macro Context

AI-crypto convergence has attracted $516 million in project funding in the first eight months of 2025 alone, up 6% from all of 2024. The global AI agents market is projected to grow from $7.84B to $52.62B between 2025 and 2030 at a 46.3% CAGR. Coinbase has launched x402 (a stablecoin payment protocol for AI agents to transact autonomously online) and a purpose-built crypto wallet for AI agents with embedded guardrails. Brian Armstrong stated on Coinbase's Q4 earnings call that "stablecoins will be the default payment method for AI agents," and Base chain's TVL has reached $12.64 billion in 2026. The on-ramps for agents to earn, spend, and compound capital now exist. What's missing is systematic identification of the agents actually doing it.

## Tier 1: Strong Candidates

*Clear revenue or rapid traction, credible team, evidence of real economic activity.*

### 1. Felix (OpenClaw)

**What it does:** Felix is a fully autonomous AI CEO operating a "zero-human company" called OpenClaw. Built on top of the Claude-based OpenClaw framework, Felix runs a suite of products: Clawmarks (a marketplace for OpenClaw skills and personas), the Felix Craft PDF product ($41,000 in PDF sales alone), and content marketing services. The agent autonomously develops products overnight, manages AI-employee sub-agents for sales and support, writes 170+ blog posts for SEO/viral marketing, and maintains a Stripe account and ETH wallet. All operating expenses — AI inference costs, hosting, tooling — are paid from the revenue Felix generates, not fronted by Nat Eliason. Felix recently gained the ability to deploy micro-agents as "employees" for enterprise clients analyzing Slack histories to identify roles replaceable by AI.

**Who built it:** Nat Eliason — founder of Growth Machine (content marketing agency serving Fortune 500s and YC startups, acquired in 2025), crypto author (*Crypto Confidential*), and serial entrepreneur. X: @nateliason.

**Revenue/traction signal:** Over $250,000 in verified revenue in less than one month of operation. Weekly Stripe revenue hit $38,554 plus $7,102 in ETH trading fees. $62,013 total revenue in first two weeks alone. Additionally holds ~$50,000 in $FELIX tokens. The PDF product ($29 price point) generated $41,000 in sales.

**Stage:** Active → Scaling fast

**Why interesting:** Clearest public proof-of-concept for the "zero-human company" model. Cross-modal payments (ETH + Stripe). Eliason plans Base chain integration for inter-agent micropayments. Transparent revenue dashboard is a structural moat. **Recruitable** — exploring OpenClaw + onchain infrastructure.

### 2. Virtuals Protocol (Ecosystem + ACP)

**What it does:** Largest AI agent economy by deployed agent count (18,000+ agents). Core innovation: tokenizing AI agents. Agent Commerce Protocol (ACP) — first full-lifecycle standard for autonomous onchain commerce. Agents discover, negotiate, delegate, escrow, evaluate, and settle in USDC without human input. Revenue Network distributes up to $1M/month to agents generating measurable service revenue.

**Who built it:** Jansen Teng and Tiew Wee Kee, Hong Kong. Founded 2021.

**Revenue/traction signal:** $1.86M in Q1 2026 (~$50K/day). A2A transaction revenue surpassed $3M (March 9, 2026). Ecosystem aGDP exceeds $470M. Developer teams collected $16.6M+ lifetime fees; protocol accumulated ~$60M in fees at ~$170K/day. Top tokens (LUNA, aixbt, G.A.M.E) hundreds of millions in market cap.

**Stage:** Scaled

**Why interesting:** Building an agent civilization — autonomous economy with payment rail, reputation layer, capital formation. ACP is winner-take-most infrastructure play. Verified $3M A2A revenue is a landmark: on-chain proof machines pay machines for real services. **Recruitable as LPs or ecosystem partners.**
